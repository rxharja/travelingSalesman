The Traveling Salesman

Three Approaches

A salesman must visit these ten cities on our black map. He needs the shortest possible route. It's obvious which approach generates the best solution.


Random

Random configurations are tried, by shuffling around the cities. The best is displayed in blue.

Lexicographic

All possibilities are ordered lexicgraphically and visited one after another. Computationally expensive to see every single possibility for larger numbers of cities. The best is displayed in yellow

Genetic

Uses a genetic algorithm approach to heuristically solve for the shortest path. The best is displayed in red and the best of that generation is displayed in white.

Try your own!

Click on the canvas to drop a city on the map. When you're satisfied with the number of cities and locations, click run. Otherwise hit reset to start over. If you can't be bothered, simply hit random to generate a random configuration. Make sure to mess around with the sliders!
